Pokémon Mechanics

Catchable Species

A total of 650 Pokémon are available, covering all Pokémon up to Generation V, plus Enamorus to complete the seasonal quartet.

All Pokémon, moves and items use Generation V data. This includes:

  • Personal information
  • Learnsets
  • Items effects
  • Move mechanics
  • New and Hidden Abilities

For authoritative reference and verification, consult Bulbapedia.

Encounter Methods

Pokémon can be encountered using multiple methods:

  • Walking in tall grass
  • Exploring dungeons
  • Surfing water bodies
  • Using the Fishing Rod
  • Interacting with them in the environment

In addition:

  • Some trainers offer in-game trades.
  • Certain special Pokémon can be obtained as Gifts or by fulfilling different criteria.
  • Fossil Pokémon can be found in Digging Spots and revived at the Fossil Caravan.

Rare Pokémon can only be encountered under specific conditions:

  • If you have more than one Pokémon in your party, Wild Double Battles may occur. In these battles, a special species unique to that location has a 5% chance of appearing.
  • Fishing Spots, found across various bodies of water, allow special encounters when using the Fishing Rod. These also have a 5% chance of yielding a rare species.

Pokémon obtained through these methods are marked in the documentation accordingly.

Experience Formula

The experience calculation is based on Generation V but simplified and rebalanced to reward effort more directly.

Standard Multipliers

  • x1.5 for Trainer Battles
  • x1.5 if the Pokémon was received via trade
  • x1.5 if the Pokémon holds an experience-boosting item (e.g., Lucky Egg)

Effort Multiplier

The level-based effort multiplier is calculated as:

  • L_v: Level of the victorious Pokémon
  • L_s: Level of the defeated Pokémon
  • B: Baseline value, set to 0.1
  • F: Linear flattening coefficient, set to 15

Notes

  • When L_v = L_s, numerator and denominator are equal; the multiplier becomes 1 + B, granting a slight bonus to experience.
  • Defeating a higher-level Pokémon grants a larger experience bonus, scaling with the level gap according to the formula above.
