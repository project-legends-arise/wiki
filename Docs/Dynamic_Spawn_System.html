Dynamic Spawn System

The Dynamic Spawn System governs recurring overworld events that grant access to special items and unique encounter opportunities. These events are triggered by simply moving around within an area, and only one dynamic spawn can be active at a time.

An active spawn remains present until it is interacted with, then disappears. It also vanishes if the player leaves the area, collects another overworld item, engages in a wild battle, or interacts with an NPC. Repels do not prevent dynamic spawns from appearing. Active spawns are not stored when saving the game.

Each spawn type has a distinct visual cue, sometimes accompanied by sound effects, allowing the player to identify it before approaching.

Spawn Types

  • GRASS SPARKLES: Grass Sparkles occasionally appear in tall grass while exploring routes and forests. Interacting with one grants an item with the following odds: 75% chance to receive a Season Coin matching the current in-game season, or 25% chance to receive a Berry, whose type depends on the local Climate Zone.
    • For more information about Berry type distribution, visit the following article: Climate System.
  • MINING SPOTS: Mining Spots appear in selected caves after obtaining the Mining Gear from the Mining Maniac in the house on Route 207, just above Oreburgh City. They appear as cracks on cave walls and can be excavated.
    • For more information, visit the following article: Mining Gear.
  • RIPPLING WATER: Rippling water appears only on water tiles. Surfing or fishing on these tiles allows encounters with Pokemon species that are not available through standard water encounters.
    • Information about which Pokemon can be found with this method is available on the corresponding location page.
  • OVERHEAD SHADOWS: Overhead Shadows appear as dark circles on the ground. Stepping on one has a 90% chance of yielding a Feather item and a 10% chance of triggering an encounter with a Pok?mon species that cannot normally be found in that area through standard encounters.
    • Information about which Pokemon can be found with this method is available on the corresponding location page.
  • WISPS: Wisps are small purple ghostly flames hovering just above the ground. They appear rarely at night in eerie locations such as Solaceon Town and Solitary Hill. They can be traded for rare items with specific NPCs, including Hex Maniacs and Clerics, who actively seek them.
    • Collecting 108 Wisps and offering them to the keystone-shaped rock at Solitary Hill summons Spiritomb. This event can be repeated indefinitely, as long as the required number of Wisps is gathered each time.

During Spacetime Distortions, the system can also generate the following event types:

  • FRENZY POKEMON: A Pokemon with the Frenzy status effect will immediately attack when approached. Frenzy Pokemon always have their Hidden Ability.
  • DUST CLOUDS: A brief dust-like smoke effect marks a temporary item spawn. Stepping onto it immediately grants the item.
